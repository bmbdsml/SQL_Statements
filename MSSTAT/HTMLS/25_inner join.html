Inner Join (Examples)


 Example 1: Orders & Customers 

1. Orders table:

	   | order_id | customer_id | order_date | total_amount |
	   |----------|-------------|------------|--------------|
	   | 1        | 101         | 2025-01-01 | 500          |
	   | 2        | 102         | 2025-01-02 | 150          |
	   | 3        | 103         | 2025-01-03 | 200          |

2. Customers table:

	   | customer_id | customer_name | email             |
	   |-------------|---------------|-------------------|
	   | 101         | John Doe      | john@example.com  |
	   | 102         | Jane Smith    | jane@example.com  |
	   | 103         | Sam Brown     | sam@example.com   |

    To list the order details along with customer names:

	SELECT Orders.order_id, Customers.customer_name, Orders.total_amount
	
	FROM Orders 
	
	INNER JOIN Customers
	
	ON Orders.customer_id = Customers.customer_id;

     Result:

	| order_id | customer_name | total_amount |
	|----------|---------------|--------------|
	| 1        | John Doe      | 500          |
	| 2        | Jane Smith    | 150          |
	| 3        | Sam Brown     | 200          |

  Example 2: Joining Products and Categories 

	1. Products table:

	   | product_id | product_name | category_id | price |
	   |------------|--------------|-------------|-------|
	   | 1          | Laptop       | 1           | 1000  |
	   | 2          | Phone        | 2           | 700   |
	   | 3          | Tablet       | 1           | 300   |
	
	2. Categories table:

	   | category_id | category_name |
	   |-------------|---------------|
	   | 1           | Electronics   |
	   | 2           | Mobile        |

	To get the product names with their category names:


	SELECT Products.product_name, Categories.category_name
	
	FROM Products
	
	INNER JOIN Categories
	
	ON Products.category_id = Categories.category_id;

      Result:

	| product_name | category_name |
	|--------------|---------------|
	| Laptop       | Electronics   |
	| Phone        | Mobile        |
	| Tablet       | Electronics   |


 Example 3: Joining Employees and Departments with Additional Filters 

	1. Employees table:
	
	   | employee_id | name      | department_id |
	   |-------------|-----------|---------------|
	   | 1           | Alice     | 101           |
	   | 2           | Bob       | 102           |
	   | 3           | Charlie   | 101           |
	   | 4           | David     | 103           |

	2. Departments table:

	   | department_id | department_name |
	   |---------------|-----------------|
	   | 101           | HR              |
	   | 102           | IT              |
	   | 103           | Finance         |

	SELECT Employees.name, Departments.department_name
	
	FROM Employees
	
	INNER JOIN Departments
	
	ON Employees.department_id = Departments.department_id
	
	WHERE Departments.department_name IN ('HR', 'IT');

      Result:

	| name    | department_name |
	|---------|-----------------|
	| Alice   | HR              |
	| Bob     | IT              |
	| Charlie | HR              |
